CURRENT PROJECTS

EXPERIMENTAL FINANCE Experimentation in finance serves two crucial purposes. Firstly, it allows for scientific evaluation, preventing us from straying into speculative territory without empirical validation. Good experiments, like good theories, require isolating phenomena and disregarding complicating factors. Realism is not the primary concern; rather, it's about understanding underlying principles. Secondly, experimentation deepens our comprehension of theory. Through conducting experiments, we gain insights and appreciate the intricacies and elegance of the theory. As Richard Feynman put it, theory comprehension necessitates the ability to create it. Learn More

DECISION NEUROSCIENCE Financial risks are a recent evolutionary development, and our brain may not have adapted perfectly to handle them. Initially, we aim to understand how the brain perceives risks and makes decisions in controlled but ecologically relevant situations. While we have gained knowledge about reward learning and the brain's innate ability to employ temporal difference modeling, our team emphasizes the importance of studying risk perception and learning. These aspects are crucial for any organism that is sensitive to risks, regardless of whether they lean towards risk aversion or risk-seeking behavior. We also delve into belief formation concerning events that are not yet associated with rewards or losses, such as predicting snowfall without knowing if it will lead to skiing enjoyment or the risk of accidents on slippery roads. Furthermore, we explore strategic uncertainty, which arises when we lack knowledge of an opponent's actions but those actions carry significance, such as a soccer player taking a penalty or a baseball pitcher trying to deceive the batter. Game theory provides a formal framework to investigate strategic uncertainty, an essential element of what psychologists refer to as theory of mind. Learn More

EXPERIMENTAL FINANCE WORLD MEETINGS - SEF

The World Meetings of the Society for Experimental Finance 2023 will be held in Sofia. 'Lucas' in the Laboratory

This study examines the Lucas asset pricing model in a controlled setting. Participants trade securities, aligning prices with consumption betas and aggregate dividends. However, excessive price volatility and heterogeneous choices challenge traditional tests. These findings emphasize the complexities of asset pricing dynamics and the need for further research.
